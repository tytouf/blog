Open Data and a map of traffic accident (France)

jeu. 29 mars 2012
By Christophe Augier

In Open Data.

tags: open-dataleafletpython

Translations: fr

Open Data is a beautiful idea: make data public and available to anyone. Yet while this idea is very honorable, without tools to sort, cross-reference, match and visualize data it loses its meaning.

With this in my mind I went to see what kind of datasets and tools where published on data.gouv.fr, a website opened recently by the French government. I found some interesting dataset related to traffic accident and decided to work on a map to display the localization of the accidents. I developed a python script to extract the data from the ODS files and format them so that I can display them on a map rendered using Leaflet. More details (sorry in French) and the map here, the tools are licensed under the Creative Commons.
